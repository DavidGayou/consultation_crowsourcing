In terms of conceptual terminology and legal contents, the term "Intellectual Property" can be in
simpler form defined as a possession of intangible nature, which results from process of human
thinking. We can include there original ideas, themes, manuals and solutions for changing the
surrounding or internal world of human being
  It looks like an embodiment of ideal!
  But what is the reality?
There are even several renowned institutions boasting by their orientation in the field of
"Intellectual Property". The most known, and seemingly the most competent is the WIPO (World
Intellectual Property Organization - Svetová organizácia duševného vlastníctva), established by
"Agreement" of contractual parties, signed in Stockholm on July 14th, 1967, where also previous
ČSSR joined on Sept 22nd, 1970. Currently it has 184 members (states) throughout the world.
Another institution is the EPO (European Patent Organization), which actually contains 38 member
states from Europe area. Then also DPP (Dohovor o patentovom práve - Agreement on Patent
Law) with quasi regional sphere of action, and so on.

  But how, and whose interests these institutions do protect?9
  From standpoint of creative focus and value orientation, it is possible to divide the creators -
authors, who produce works belonging into sphere of "Intellectual Property", simply into two
categories.

   The first category consists of creators, authors, of art named by me humanitarian orientation.
 Literary men, musicians, painters, sculptors, architects, movie and theatre artists, radio and TV
 creators, and similar.
 Thus category of creators, altogether in all cases acting in unproductive, tertiary sphere of each
 social formation, and thus undoubtedly of all the civilization (however, with practically unreal
 privileges, guaranteed in Slovak law system bv the Law no. 618/2003 of Coll. - so called Author's
 Law), where they by means of their works contribute into monetary circulation practically from
 pocket to pocket, or from one account to the other respectively, without any evident addition of
 value with respect to the social, and thereby civilizing progression.
   Their works don't underlie nearly any institutional registration (see par. 19 of Directive
 2004/48/ES European Parliament and Council from Apr 29th, 2004 - on enforceability of
 Intellectual Property's rights) - quotation:
                         "Since the copyright exists from the creation of work, and doesn't
                  require formal registration, it is necessary .. .and so on "
It is sufficient that they authorize work by their signature and even this guarantees them lifetime
(even 70 years after their death) copyright, and from this resulting material law for appropriate
author's reward!!
And that is valid for everybody, without any difference. Also for one who created unique, also for
one, who created kitsch or trash.
For this purpose they have their own institutional protectors, or rather patrons. In Slovak
circumstances I can mention only the SOZA. which initiatively (sometimes even on the edge of
law) executes the function of collector of "director's fees" practically even from musically talented
folklore artists whistling at public toilets!9
