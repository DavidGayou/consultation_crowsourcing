21.	          Are	  there	  problems	  arising	  from	  the	  fact	  that	  most	  limitations	  and	  exceptions	  
provided	  in	  the	  EU	  copyright	  directives	  are	  optional	  for	  the	  Member	  States?	  	  

YES.	  Cross	  border	  services	  demand	  a	  harmonized	  pan-­‐European	  legal	  framework	  and	  the	  
adjustment	  of	  the	  legal	  environment	  to	  the	  development	  of	  the	  Information	  Society.	  

In	   particular,	   the	   exceptions	   should	   not	   be	   defined	   in	   an	   exhaustive	   list	   but	   in	   a	   flexible	   way	  
which	   allows	   the	   adjustment	   required	   by	   a	   fast	   evolving	   environment	   for	   content,	   creation,	  
access	  and	  distribution.

64.	                                                      In	  your	  view,	  is	  there	  a	  need	  to	  clarify	  at	  the	  EU	  level	  the	  scope	  and	  application	  of	  
the	  private	  copying	  and	  reprography	  exceptions1	  in	  the	  digital	  environment?	  

YES.	  APRITEL	  believes	  there	  are	  certain	  misunderstandings	  at	  national	  level	  which	  could	  
benefit	  from	  EU	  level	  clarification.	  

In	  effect,	  in	  Portugal,	  there	  have	  been	  legislative	  proposals	  to	  amend	  the	  Private	  Copy	  Law	  in	  
force	  where	  existing	  levies	  would	  be	  raised	  but	  also	  additional	  storage	  products	  would	  also	  
be	  levied.	  These	  included	  USB	  Pens,	  Mobile	  Telephones	  and	  other	  devices	  that	  clearly,	  due	  
to	  their	  usage,	  go	  beyond	  the	  scope	  of	  what	  the	  private	  copying	  exception.	  In	  the	  case	  of	  
USB	  pens,	  these	  products	  are	  used	  by	  consumers	  typically	  for	  transporting	  documents	  
(USBs)	  and	  in	  the	  case	  of	  Mobile	  Phones,	  for	  storing	  personal	  pictures	  and	  videos.	  

In	  addition	  to	  the	  above,	  the	  mentioned	  proposals	  also	  envisaged	  taxation	  of	  Mp3	  readers	  
and	  Set-­‐Top-­‐Boxes.	  In	  these	  cases,	  said	  taxation	  would	  lead	  to	  double	  taxation	  and	  a	  
problem	  which	  has	  been	  recognized	  in	  the	  2013	  recommendations	  of	  Antonio	  Vitorino	  on	  
Copyright	  Levies:	  “A	  person	  paying	  for	  e.g.	  the	  download	  of	  a	  song	  expects	  that	  this	  
payment	  does	  not	  only	  cover	  the	  first	  download	  of	  that	  song	  onto	  his	  or	  her	  personal	  
computer	  but	  also	  the	  subsequent	  copying	  of	  that	  song	  to	  a	  certain	  number	  of	  his	  or	  her	  
mobile	  devices,	  as	  determined	  by	  the	  usage	  rules	  of	  the	  service	  provider.	  Similarly,	  a	  person	  
subscribing	  to	  a	  music	  streaming	  service	  is	  usually	  not	  only	  paying	  for	  the	  online	  streaming	  
to	  his	  or	  her	  device/s	  but	  also	  for	  the	  possibility	  to	  create	  "playlists"	  that	  can	  be	  listened	  to	  
when	  "offline".	  	  […]”.

Additional	  remuneration	  under	  the	  guise	  of	  ‘private	  copying’	  levies	  is	  not	  acceptable.	  

The	  fact	  is	  there	  seems	  to	  be	  some	  confusion	  in	  the	  public	  debate	  between	  private	  copy	  and	  
misuse	  of	  copyrights:	  increasing	  levies	  on	  private	  copying	  is	  often	  regarded	  as	  a	  remedy	  for	  
the	  abuse	  of	  third	  party	  copy	  and	  neighbouring	  rights	  in	  the	  digital	  environment.	  These	  are	  
two	  very	  different	  issues	  that	  must	  be	  treated	  separately.	  

For	  the	  above	  reasons,	  in	  APRITEL’s	  view,	  failing	  to	  clearly	  defining	  private	  copying	  (and	  
ultimately	  its	  exceptions)	  will	  inevitably	  lead	  to	  an	  unfair	  treatment	  of	  legitimate	  users	  of	  
private	  copying	  and	  ultimately	  hamper	  intellectual	  creation	  by	  artificially	  increasing	  the	  cost	  
for	  end	  users	  of	  essential	  tools	  such	  as	  digital	  storage	  systems.	  

65.	           Should	  digital	  copies	  made	  by	  end	  users	  for	  private	  purposes	  in	  the	  context	  of	  a	  
service	  that	  has	  been	  licensed	  by	  rightholders,	  and	  where	  the	  harm	  to	  the	  rightholder	  is	  
minimal,	  be	  subject	  to	  private	  copying	  levies?	  

NO.	  When	  acquiring	  a	  license	  the	  user	  has	  reasonable	  expectations	  as	  to	  its	  ability	  to	  use	  the	  
copyrighted	  work	  which	  should	  be	  taken	  into	  account.	  Further	  fees	  should	  be	  deemed	  
unreasonable.	  

66.	          How	  would	  changes	  in	  levies	  with	  respect	  to	  the	  application	  to	  	  online	  services	  (e.g.	  
services	  based	  on	  cloud	  computing	  	  allowing,	  for	  instance,	  users	  to	  have	  copies	  on	  
different	  devices)	  impact	  the	  development	  and	  functioning	  of	  new	  business	  models	  on	  the	  
one	  hand	  and	  rightholders’	  revenue	  on	  the	  other?	  	  

APRITEL	  and	  its	  members	  believe	  that	  the	  increase	  of	  services	  or	  equipments	  subject	  to	  
levies	  will	  have	  negative	  impact	  on	  the	  price	  of	  such	  services,	  their	  competitiveness	  and,	  
consequently,	  on	  the	  extent	  of	  investment	  that	  the	  market	  is	  available	  to	  make.
